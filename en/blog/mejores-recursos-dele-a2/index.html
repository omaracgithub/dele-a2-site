Best resources to prepare for the DELE A2 in 2026

Updated in June 2026 · 9 min read

Preparing for the DELE A2 does not require spending hundreds of euros on academies. With the right resources and an organized study plan, you can pass from home. Here is an updated selection of the best materials available in 2026, organized by category.

Preparation Books

1. Preparation for DELE A2 — Edelsa Publishing

The quintessential reference book. Includes complete exam models with the exact format of the Instituto Cervantes, downloadable audios, and answer keys. It is the closest thing to the real exam you will find on paper.

Ideal for: The last 4 weeks before the exam, to practice with the real format.

2. Objetivo DELE A2 — SGEL Publishing

More didactic than Edelsa: explains the strategies for each task before practicing. Includes specific tips and examples of answers for the expression tests. A good option if you want to understand the "why" behind each exercise.

3. The Keys to DELE A2 — Difusión

Focused on tricks and strategies rather than the volume of exercises. Useful as a complement to Edelsa, not as a standalone preparation book.

Mobile Apps

1. Prep DELE (iOS)

App specifically designed for the DELE A2 with complete exam models, real audios, automatic correction of reading and listening, and writing evaluation with AI. Identical format to the official exam. Quick practice by sections or complete timed exams.

2. Anki (flashcards)

Not specific to DELE but is the best spaced repetition tool for vocabulary. Look for shared decks of "DELE A2 vocabulary" or create your own with the words you are learning.

3. SpanishDict

Dictionary with complete conjugations, pronunciation, and examples in context. Useful as a quick reference when studying and you don't remember a conjugation or preposition.

YouTube Channels

1. Español con Juan

Short videos on grammar and vocabulary explained clearly and entertainingly. Has a specific series for A1-A2 levels. Subtitles available.

2. Dreaming Spanish

Method of "comprehensible input": videos where they speak slowly using gestures and images so you can understand without translating. Ideal for training your ear naturally. Has videos filtered by level.

3. ProfeDeELE

Channel with interactive exercises, grammatical explanations, and specific videos about the DELE tests. Content organized by levels.

Podcasts for A2 Level

1. Notes in Spanish (Inspired Beginners)

Natural but slow conversations between Ben (English) and Marina (Spanish). Perfect A2 level. Episodes of 10-15 minutes with transcripts available.

2. Español Automático

Karo Martínez speaks slowly and repeats ideas in different ways. Ideal for passive listening while walking or cooking.

3. Coffee Break Spanish

Podcast structured like a course. Season 2 corresponds approximately to A2 level. Explains grammar in context with real dialogues.

Free Resources from Instituto Cervantes

  • AVE Global: Official Cervantes platform with interactive exercises by level (some modules are free)
  • Exam Models: The Institute publishes a free exam model for each level on its official website (examenes.cervantes.es)
  • CVC (Centro Virtual Cervantes): Grammar, vocabulary, and culture activities organized by levels

How to Combine Resources Effectively

Do not use everything at once. Here is a combination plan for 8 weeks:

  • Weeks 1-4 (base): Grammar with YouTube (ProfeDeELE) + vocabulary with Anki + daily listening with podcast (15 min)
  • Weeks 5-6 (format): Edelsa book (1 model per week) + Prep DELE app for daily quick practice
  • Weeks 7-8 (intensive): Complete timed simulations + review of mistakes + speaking practice by recording yourself

What You DO NOT Need

  • A physical academy (useful but not essential for A2)
  • More than 2 textbooks (one for exercises + one for models is sufficient)
  • Gamification apps like Duolingo (entertaining but do not prepare for the DELE format)
  • Private tutor (helps for the oral test, but is not mandatory)
