What is the DELE A2 and what is it for? — Complete Guide 2026

Updated in June 2026 · 12 min read

What is the DELE A2?

The DELE A2 (Diploma of Spanish as a Foreign Language, level A2) is an official certificate issued by the Instituto Cervantes on behalf of the Ministry of Education of Spain. It certifies that the candidate can communicate in everyday situations using frequently used expressions and basic vocabulary.

The A2 level corresponds to the basic user according to the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR). Once obtained, the diploma does not expire: it has permanent validity worldwide.

What is the DELE A2 for?

Obtaining Spanish nationality

Since November 2015, anyone applying for Spanish nationality by residence must prove a minimum level of Spanish A2. The DELE A2 is the most direct way to meet this requirement. It is issued by the Instituto Cervantes and is automatically accepted by the Ministry of Justice.

If your native language is not Spanish and you have been living in Spain for the required time (generally 10 years, 2 for Ibero-Americans, 1 for those married to a Spaniard), you will need to present the DELE A2 along with the CCSE test (Constitutional and Sociocultural Knowledge of Spain).

Residence and administrative procedures

Although it is not always legally required for residence, many immigration lawyers recommend having the DELE A2 as documentary proof of integration. It facilitates procedures with the Administration and demonstrates a willingness to integrate linguistically.

Access to the job market

In sectors such as hospitality, commerce, personal care, and services, an A2 level allows communication with clients and colleagues. The official certificate adds credibility compared to a simple self-assessment.

Foundation for further advancement

The DELE A2 represents a motivating milestone for Spanish learners. After passing it, many candidates continue to the B1 level, which opens doors to universities and qualified positions.

Structure of the DELE A2 exam

The exam consists of four tests grouped into two blocks. You must pass each block separately.

Group 1: Comprehension

Test 1 — Reading comprehension (60 minutes)

5 tasks with 25 questions. You will read short and simple texts: posters, notices, short emails, advertisements, restaurant menus, and brief news articles. You must identify specific information, grasp the main idea, or relate texts to statements.

Test 2 — Listening comprehension (35 minutes)

5 tasks with 25 questions. You will listen to everyday conversations, phone messages, public announcements, and radio snippets. Each audio is played twice. The vocabulary and speech speed are adapted to the elementary level.

Group 2: Expression

Test 3 — Written expression and interaction (50 minutes)

3 tasks: completing a form or sheet, writing a short message (60-70 words), and drafting a longer text (70-80 words). The topics are everyday: booking a hotel, writing to a friend, describing your routine, or recounting a recent experience.

Test 4 — Oral expression and interaction (12 minutes + 12 for preparation)

4 tasks performed in front of two examiners: a short prepared monologue (describing your family, job, or city), a description of a photograph, a simulated dialogue (shopping in a store, asking for information), and a free conversation about everyday topics.

Scoring system

Each group (comprehension and expression) is worth a maximum of 50 points. You need at least 30 points in each group (60%) to pass. If you pass one group but fail the other, you must retake the entire exam in another session.

The comprehension tests are graded with a template (correct/incorrect answers). The expression tests are evaluated by two certified examiners using an analytical scale that measures coherence, vocabulary, grammar, and appropriateness to the context.

Difference between DELE A2 and DELE A1

The A1 is an access level: greetings, introductions, very basic phrases. The A2 allows for simple conversations, recounting past experiences (present perfect and simple past), expressing likes, and making future plans. The jump is not huge but requires mastering verb tenses that A1 does not demand.

How many hours of study do I need?

The CEFR estimates between 180 and 200 accumulated study hours to reach an A2 level. If starting from scratch, this equates to about 6-8 months of regular study (3-4 hours per week). If you already have basic knowledge or live in a Spanish-speaking environment, you can prepare for the exam in 2-3 months with daily dedication.

Exam dates and sessions 2026

The Instituto Cervantes offers between 6 and 8 annual sessions for the DELE A2:

  • February: limited session
  • April: general session
  • May: general session (the most popular)
  • July: general session
  • September: session in some centers
  • October: general session
  • November: general session

Registration closes 3-6 weeks before the exam. Check with your local examination center for exact deadlines.

Price of the DELE A2 exam in 2026

In Spain, the fee is around €130. In other countries, it may vary (between €100 and €200 depending on the center). The fee is non-refundable if you do not show up, although some centers allow rescheduling with medical justification.

How to prepare for the DELE A2?

The key is to combine grammar study with real practice of the exam format. Familiarizing yourself with the types of tasks, managing time, and practicing oral expression are decisive factors.

Using real exam models allows you to identify weak points. Listening comprehension and oral expression are often the tests that are most challenging at the A2 level because they require active practice that cannot be achieved solely with textbooks.
